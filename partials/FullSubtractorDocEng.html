A brief theorical introduction about full subtractor
Full subtractor is based on a combinatory circuit, which is even simpler: the half subtractor. This circuit performs the subtraction of two bits which are the input, and that are the minuend and the subtrahend. Half subtractor circuit has as output, the result of the subtraction and a borrow (borrow-out)

This simple circuit is based on three logic gates: a XOR gate, a AND gate and a NOT gate. XOR calculates the subtraction, while NOT and AND gates perform the operation of determining borrow. This simple circuit is good but not enough, because it is not able to considerate an outgoing borrow from another subtraction. Therefor is not sufficient, as a half adder is not sufficient to perform n bit sum. To solve this problem we can use more half subractor in such a way that we can consider even a borrow-in.
